What is paraphrasing?

Definition: Paraphrasing rewrites a source passage in fresh wording and structure that fits your audience and purpose. It is not a synonym swap. It must be accurate and typically must be cited.

Paraphrase vs. Quote vs. Summary:

  • Paraphrase: same scope, new wording.
  • Quote: same wording, quotation marks + citation.
  • Summary: condensed key points in new wording (linking out: Paraphrase vs. Rewrite vs. Summarize).

Tone vs. Style vs. Mode

Writers often conflate these. Use them deliberately to match task requirements:

  • Tone = attitude (e.g., formal, friendly, confident, apologetic). It shapes how readers feel.
  • Style = expression norms (e.g., academic, business, casual, professional). It shapes expectations of format and precision.
  • Mode = transformation type (e.g., standard, fluent, creative, academic, expand, shorten, grammar).

Choosing the right combination keeps your paraphrase faithful, readable, and appropriate to context (a lab report versus a product page demand different decisions).

A step-by-step paraphrasing workflow

  1. Read for meaning first. Ask: What is the claim? Evidence? Limitations?
  2. Note protected terms (names, formulas, jargon) you should not change.
  3. Close the source and write a plain-language restatement.
  4. Refine tone, style, and mode for your audience (e.g., formal + academic + shorten for abstracts).
  5. Compare to the source for accuracy and structure; adjust wording and syntax.
  6. Cite the source in the required format (APA, MLA, Chicago).
  7. Final polish: check grammar, cohesion, and flow.
  • Did you change both wording and sentence structure?
  • Did you keep protected terms accurate?
  • Is meaning identical and cited?
  • Does tone + style + mode match the assignment or brand?

Ethical use & citation

Paraphrasing strengthens your voice but never erases authorship. Always cite when paraphrasing someone else‚Äôs idea. In research writing, many journals also expect page numbers for close paraphrases. When using AI, treat outputs as drafts: verify claims, keep discipline-specific terminology, and add references.

FAQ

What is ‚Äútone‚Äù in paraphrasing and why does it matter?
Tone is the writer‚Äôs attitude (e.g., formal, friendly, confident). Matching tone to context increases trust and readability-for example, formal for journals, friendly for onboarding docs.
Is paraphrasing with AI allowed in academia?
Policies vary. Many institutions allow support tools if you maintain authorship, verify accuracy, and cite original sources. Always check your syllabus or journal guidelines.
How do I choose between expand and shorten modes?
Use shorten for abstracts, meta descriptions, or tight word counts. Use expand to unpack dense claims into clearer steps or to add transitions-without adding new, unsupported content.
What‚Äôs the fastest way to avoid plagiarism when paraphrasing?
Close the source, restate the idea from memory, then reopen and compare for accuracy. Change structure, not just words, and add a citation. Protected words should remain unchanged.

Paraphrasing is a craft: align tone, style, and mode with your goals; preserve meaning; and cite appropriately. Use tools to accelerate-not replace-critical reading and source integrity.
